There is a grid of cells similar to Connect Four, but it's only 3x3.
Players (red and black) take turns dropping their discs into one of the three slots;
 of course, if the column is full players must choose another one.
There are five discs of each colour; these are a resource and should be tracked on screen in some way.

Instead of dropping a disc, players may instead shift a row to the left or to the right.
This action physically moves the discs in that row over by one. Gravity then ensues in all columns.
It should be noted that the rows may only be moved by one from their original position; that is:
 if the top row is shifted left, then it may not be shifted left anymore until it is shifted right.
If a disc is shifted out of the playing field, such as by being in the left column preceding a left shift,
 then it falls out of the board, and is reclaimed by the player to be played on a subsequent turn.

The winner is the first player to get 3 of their colour in a row.
It is possible for both players to win simultaneously.
